Bahrain’s human-rights report: The king’s risky move
00:00 11/24/2011, The Economist: Middle East and Africa

Bassiouni dogs the king
IT WAS both a humiliation and a triumph. Bahrain’s King Hamad bin Isa al-Khalifa listened quietly as a litany of human-rights abuses committed by his security forces was read out. It was a devastating and embarrassing indictment of his security forces’ behaviour. But it was also a vindication of his claim that it would be an independent and genuine attempt to get at the truth, thereby undermining his opponents’ insistence that it would be a worthless document.Five months after the king appointed a panel of human-rights experts to examine events in Bahrain earlier this year, when anti-government protests by the majority Shias across the Sunni-ruled kingdom prompted a brutal crackdown, the Bahrain Independent Commission of Inquiry has laid bare the scale of abuse.
