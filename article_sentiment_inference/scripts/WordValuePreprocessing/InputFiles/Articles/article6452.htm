Dow Slumps on Eurozone Fears
16:22 11/23/2011, twocents@thestreet.com (Andrea Tse and Chao Deng), ^gspc, ^ixic, aa, bac, cvx, de, equities, grpn, hpq, jpm, markets, mrk, p, the market story, tivo, top, video inside, TheStreet.com

NEW YORK (TheStreet) -- Investors lost their appetite for stocks Wednesday after Europe's strongest economy -- Germany -- had trouble borrowing funds, illustrating fresh erosion of investor confidence in the region.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 236 points, or 2.1%, to finish at a session low of 11,258, marking its fifth drop in six sessions. The blue-chip index is now off 4.6% on the week. All 30 Dow components closed in the red with Bank of America, Alcoa , JPMorgan Chase and Hewlett-Packard leading the losses.

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq both suffered a sixth consecutive declines. The S&P 500 sank 26 points, or 2.2%, to settle at 1162, while the Nasdaq surrendered 61 points, or 2.4%, to close at 2460. ...
