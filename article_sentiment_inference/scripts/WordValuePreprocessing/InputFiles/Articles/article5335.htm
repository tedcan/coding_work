As the chart shows, the proportion of young people who have used cannabis in the past year continues to decline from a peak in 1998, according to the British Crime Survey (BCS). Use of class A drugs such as cocaine and heroin has not declined as much, but prior-year use of powder cocaine fell by a third in the two years from 2008-09. Figures from the National Treatment Agency suggest that heroin and crack cocaine are mostly confined to an ageing cohort.
