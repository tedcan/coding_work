SIR – Your report on the recent security situation in northern Kosovo appeared to imply that the government of Kosovo acted provocatively in response to a postponement of talks by Serbia (“A little local difficulty”, August 6th). In fact, our intention is to ensure that normal border controls are operational and that customs regulations are respected. Criminality, including the trafficking of human beings and narcotics, flourishes when the rule of law is weak or absent. Kosovo is determined to ensure that laws are applied and criminals are deterred.We remain committed to the normalisation of bilateral relations with Serbia. As you noted, exports from Kosovo have been blocked from clearing customs by Serbia since 2008, an unjustified state of affairs that we deplore.
