SIR – You looked at the factors behind why “Britain has produced too few world-class technology firms” (“Start me up”, August 6th). What you did not discuss is Britain’s leading position in university intellectual-property commercialisation. Although British venture capital and early-stage investments have withered over the past decade, some companies have used partnerships with British universities through the stockmarket to provide investors with access to enterprises built on the back of British research.Such companies include Imperial Innovations, Fusion IP and IP Group, which together boast a market capitalisation of more than £450m ($740m). These companies use financial innovation to fuel the technological sort.
