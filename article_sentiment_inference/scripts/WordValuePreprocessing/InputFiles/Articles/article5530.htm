Across the Land and the Water: Selected Poems 1964-2001. By W.G. Sebald, translated by Iain Galbraith. Hamish Hamilton; 240 pages; £14.99. To be published in America in April by Random House; $25. Buy from Amazon.com, Amazon.co.ukSINCE W.G. Sebald’s sudden death in 2001, the cult of the Britain-based German writer has spread fast. Known for his exquisite prose works that, in their combination of the real with the fictional, push at the limits of what novels can be, he is considered one of the foremost German writers of his generation. He was also a poet.“Across the Land and the Water” brings together a selection of the poems he never published in book form, if at all. Translated by Iain Galbraith, the volume sketches out a life on the move. Stretching over 37 years, the volume includes poems that Mr Galbraith found jotted down in Sebald’s archives on scraps of paper...
