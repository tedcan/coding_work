China stock outlook now bullish: analyst
04:06 11/24/2011, MarketWatch.com - MarketPulse

HONG KONG (MarketWatch) -- China, no longer worried about inflows of hot money, is set to lower the amount of funds banks are required to set aside as reserves at the central bank, according to one analyst, who said the development is reason to be upbeat on Chinese markets. Jefferies strategist Sean Darby in Hong Kong said he's been bullish on Chinese markets since Nov. 7 in expectation of a drop in working-capital funding costs. China, he said, is setting the stage for a lowering of banks' required reserves - a monetary adjustment tool which is used to regulate credit growth. Expect private investment in productivity-enhancing equipment to emerge as a new profit driver as a result of the changes, Darby said.
