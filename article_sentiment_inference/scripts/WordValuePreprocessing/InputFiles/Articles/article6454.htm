While Most Investors Flee, One Group Is Buying
15:59 11/23/2011, twocents@thestreet.com (CNBC), economy, exclude from feeds, markets, TheStreet.com

By Jeff Cox, Senior Markets Writer at CNBC.com NEW YORK (CNBC) -- Though the stock market has been unable to make a clean break into positive territory this year, there still is hope that 2011 can end on a positive note. Just don't count on mom-and-pop investors to make it happen.

Retail money has been out of the market for most of the year -- even during some of Wall Street's most violent rallies -- and is largely expected to stay there until the debt and deficit problems in the U.S. and throughout Europe get resolved.

"The retail investor is just fed up," says Brian Gendreau, market strategist with the Financial Network, an El Segundo, Calif.-based independent broker-dealer. "They don't know how they can plan for anything like retirement or buying a home. They're really put off by the volatility."
