Euro on 'Death Watch' After Investors Spurn German Bonds
15:43 11/23/2011, twocents@thestreet.com (CNBC), currencies, exclude from feeds, world markets, TheStreet.com

By John Melloy, Executive Producer for CNBC's Fast Money and Strategy Session NEW YORK (CNBC) -- Investors began to fear the worst for the euro after unusually weak demand at an auction for bonds from Germany, the region's largest economy. One analyst went so far as to put the currency on a "death watch."

Germany sold just 60% of the 6 billion euros in 10-year bunds it brought to auction, about the weakest demand seen for the country's debt in the currency's 16-year history, economists said.
